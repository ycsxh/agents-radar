## 3. Trend Signal Analysis
Today’s data confirms a decisive shift from “chat with a model” to **composable, tool-augmented agent ecosystems**. The trending list is dominated by agent-*enabling* projects, not new base models. Repositories like **agent-skills**, **awesome-design-md**, and **OfficeCLI** represent a new wave of **standardised skill packages** and **domain-specific instructions** that transform general-purpose coding agents (Claude Code, Cursor, Codex) into specialised engineers or office workers with minimal human intervention. The enormous rise of **ai-job-search** (+3716) shows that the most tangible agentic application right now is job hunting – an inherently multi-step, document-heavy workflow that benefits enormously from end-to-end automation.

A second clear signal is the **extension of agent modalities**. **claude-video** gives Claude the ability to parse video content, while **pocket-tts** delivers high-quality speech synthesis on local hardware. Together with the existing wave of vision-capable models, this points toward agents that seamlessly integrate text, image, video, and speech – a prerequisite for real-world deployment.

The persistent popularity of **system prompt leaks** (+1125 today, 55k total) reflects a community obsessed with understanding and reverse-engineering the behaviour of closed-source models. This has direct implications for prompt engineering, jailbreaking, and building “uncensored” agent forks, and it aligns with recent industry reports of increasing cat-and-mouse games between model providers and users.

Additionally, the strong presence of **RAG and memory projects** (mem0, Claude-Mem, Graphify, headroom) indicates that the community is solving the next bottleneck: persistent, efficient, and structured context for agents. The goal is no longer just retrieval, but **long-term, context-aware memory** that makes agents useful beyond single-turn interactions.

Finally, the fusion of coding agents with **design systems** (awesome-design-md) and **Office automation** (OfficeCLI) signals that AI tooling is now eating into the enterprise productivity stack directly, with open-source implementations leading the charge.
